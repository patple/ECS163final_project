Video Game Trends in Publishers and Generes From 1980 to 2016

What is Our Goal

This is an interactive slide show aimmed to show you the video game trends over the years espically the trends in publishers and generes around the world. Since our group members have personal connections with games we found it interesting to share this data with you.

The data set we will presenting was gathered from kaggle and records the sales of over 16,500 video games from 1980 to 2016. This data set is has many componets to it such as the the game title, when it was released, it's publishers and sales in each major region. However 16,500 video games are way to many to display with the visualiazations we are showing you today. We have trimmed the list down to the top 2000 games by sales.

What is a Stream Graph?

A stream graph is a type of stacked area graph where the information is usally displayed around the central axis. This allows for a flowing shape that can show certain trends. We thought this would be a good visualization to display the change of video game genres and it's publishers over time. These graphs will help you see which publishers and genre where popular during each time period.

Key Insights About Publisher Sales in North America

From the early years it seems that Nintendo dominated the gaming space in the North American market. Around the early 1990s we started to see new publishers growing. Some notable publishers are Electronic Arts and Activation which are both American publishers while Nintendo is Japanese. However in the early 2000s the popularity of Nintendo rose again and seems to die down in the 2010s.

Key Insights About Publisher Sales in Europe

Similar to the North American Graph we see a strong dominance by Nintendo in the early years of gaming with a large spike in the early 2000s. There are some differences such as Ubisoft, a French Publisher being in the top 5 publishers. However the space is still being dominated by Nintendo, EA, and Activation.

Key Insights About Publisher Sales in Japan

This time we see a very strong and consistent presence from Nintendo. Also 9 out of the publishers displayed are all domestic publishers while EA being placed 10th is a North American publisher. This could represent that the Japanese region preferes domestic game publishers over foreign ones.

Key Insights About Publisher Sales in Other Countries

This graph shows the sales in the non major regions we can see that EA is at the top of the sales instead of Nintendo and there seems to be a more even distribution of sales between the publishers
