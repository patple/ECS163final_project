Video Game Trends in Publishers and Generes From 1980 to 2016

What is Our Goal

This is an interactive slide show aimmed to show you the video game trends over the years especially the trends in publishers and generes around the world. Since our group members have personal connections with games we found it interesting to share this data with you.

About Our Data

The data set we will presenting was gathered from kaggle and records the sales of over 16,500 video games from 1980 to 2016. This data set is has many componets to it such as the the game title, when it was released, it's publishers and sales in each major region. However 16,500 video games are way to many to display with the visualiazations we are showing you today. We have trimmed the list down to the top 2000 games by sales.

What is a Stream Graph?

A stream graph is a type of stacked area graph where the information is usally displayed around the central axis. This allows for a flowing shape that can show certain trends. We thought this would be a good visualization to display the change of video game genres and it's publishers over time. These graphs will help you see which publishers and genre where popular during each time period.

How to Read a Stream Graph

  1. Identify the X-axis: Since our X-axis is time we read the graph from left to right.
  2. Identify the Y-axis : Since our Y-axis is sales in the millions it shows the total vlaue at each time point. Also there are no negative values on the Y-axis.
  3. The Streams: Each stream is a specific category which is color coded.
  4. Thickness of Streams: thicker means more during that time period.
  5. Follow a Stream: see how the categor changes over time.
  6. Look for Patterns: spikes, dipps, growing or shrinking show trends

Key Insights About Publisher Sales in North America

Nintendo had an early hold on the NA market, but in the mid-1990s, we started to see new American publishers like Electronic Arts and Activision gain momentum. Starting in the 2000s, the majority of the top publishers in NA seem to be American, showing a strong domestic market for video games. The exceptions, as shown in the stream graph, are Nintendo, Sega, and Ubisoft.

Key Insights About Publisher Sales in Europe

The EU market has a similar story to NA, where Nintendo had an early presence and was the sole prominent publisher until the rise of American publishers in the mid-1990s. Interestingly, the presence of video games, including Nintendo, were small in the EU region until the 2000s, when it saw a major boom in sales. This could suggest that before the 2000s, conditions were not suited for the growth of video games; perhaps Nintendo saw EU as a small market until then. EU did not have a strong domestic market, sharing many of their top publishers with NA, where the majority are American.

Key Insights About Publisher Sales in Japan

Japan has a very strong and consistent video game culture, of course being kickstarted by Nintendo. As early as the 1980s, video games have had a large presence in Japan, showing less relative growth than the other regions. Japan also has an incredible domestic market, where 9 of 10 top publishers are based in Japan, with the sole exception being Electronic Arts. The graph also shows that Japan’s video game market is heavily and consistently dominated by Nintendo. It would be literally true to say that Nintendo makes up over half of all of Japan’s video game sales.

Key Insights About Publisher Sales in Other Regions

There are a few notable things to point out about video game markets in Other regions. The rise of video gaming is slow, slower than even EU. Video game sales did not really take off until 2004, with a boom in sales between Take-Two, Nintendo, and Sony, primarily. Sales also have a smaller absolute peak than all of the other regions, capping out at 90M for Electronic Arts, compared to the other minimum of 395M for Nintendo in Japan. This could either suggest that the data we had is incomplete and misses sales from these regions, or there wasn’t a strong market for video games, whether it be by publisher choice or other conditions. This group of regions is also the only one where Nintendo is not the top 1 publisher, losing to Electronic Arts, showing EA’s willingness to appeal to foreign markets. The lack of new publishers in this area also shows the dominance of American and Japanese publishers in video game markets.

Key Insights About Publisher Sales Globally

The summed up sales for all publishers is remarkably similar to the NA region. This is possibly because of NA’s lead in absolute numbers, with a similar number of sales to every other region combined. The graph paints a clear picture of video game publisher history. Nintendo was an early giant with nearly no competition until the mid-1990s with the rise of American publishers. In the global market, Nintendo still reigns supreme, but regional publisher dominance is split between America and Japan. Japanese publishers are mostly represented with the titan Nintendo and to a smaller extent, Sony. American publishers have lesser dominance individually, but as a region, they rival Japan’s presence in the global market.

Key Insights About Genre Popularity in North America

The NA market features most prominently Action, Shooter, and Sports games. Given NA’s preference of domestic publishers and the reputation of American publishers to put out these exact genres of games, especially Electronic Arts and Activision, this is a clear connection to the publishers. The runner-up of Platformers is likely attributed to the presence of Nintendo in the market. Something very notable is that Puzzle games had a large bump in 1989 and never had significant relevance again. It would be more apt to say a singular Puzzle game, since this short period of dominance in the Puzzle genre was most likely solely caused by Tetris (1989) for the NES, showing, in a funny way, the sheer popularity of Tetris.

Key Insights About Genre Popularity in Europe

The EU market for genres is nearly identical to the NA market with only minor shifts in placements. Considering the EU market for publishers was also similar, this is another connection to the publishers. While the small changes are generally not particularly noteworthy, it is interesting to see the placement swap of Shooters and Sports in the EU region compared to NA. Looking at the numbers, this is not a fluke, since Sports are meaningfully ahead of Shooters in EU and vice versa in NA. This is a difference that could possibly be explained by a difference in culture. EU generally has a stronger sports culture than NA, while NA most definitely has a stronger gun culture than EU.

Key Insights About Genre Popularity in Japan

Similar to the publisher graph, Japan has vastly different tastes than the other regions. The Shooter genre, near the top in NA and EU, is downgraded to being one of the least popular genres in Japan. Meanwhile, Role-Playing Games dominate by far. This can be attributed to the widespread nature of Role-Playing Games in Japan, with many different Japanese publishers holding popular Role-Playing Game IPs, like Final Fantasy, Fire Emblem, and Dragon Quest. The publisher Square Enix (and before their merger as Square and Enix) is especially widely known for their dominance in Role-Playing Games, and sure enough, Japan is the only region where they are not only popular, but a leading Publisher when combining their shared works. So influential are Japanese publishers’ Role-Playing Games that there exists a subgenre known as Japanese Role-Playing Games (JRPGs) that often share common elements not found in Western Role-Playing Games.

Key Insights About Genre Popularity in Other Countries

Interestingly, the Other regions share a fairly similar genre trend to the EU region, where Action, Sports, and Shooters are ahead of the pack, in that order. This can possibly be attributed to closer cultural similarities between the EU region and the rest of the world. Something noteworthy is that Platformers are less popular outside of the major regions. This can be connected to the lack of Nintendo’s presence in these markets, since, again, these are the only markets where Nintendo is not the topmost publisher.

Key Insights About Genre Popularity in Other Countries

The summed up sales for all genres globally are fairly balanced over time. There are no extreme fluctuations that are obvious besides the Tetris bump of 1989. There are similar peaks as the other regions in game sales, especially around 2007 to 2009. Overall, this is a good graph to show the general popularity of game genres, not just over time.

Summary of Graph Analysis

Publisher Graphs

As can be easily seen, publisher trends differ a lot between regions. While some things stay sort of similar, like the presence of Nintendo and the growth of video games at the turn of the millennium, the general trends might be different. EU’s market is similar to NA, but Japan’s market is vastly different. The Other regions also have key differences that set them apart from the main regions. The stream graph does not let us easily speculate into why these trends might be different, other than what we previously know about publishers and their home markets, but the insights from this do prompt discussion and let us ask questions that could answer why trends are set.

A previous point made in the insights for EU and Other regions’ top publishers was that it could be publisher’s choice to market their games to a certain region. Indeed, we do know that publishers will prioritize regions that they see as valuable consumers for their products. Thus, the trends of publishers in regions could possibly be as arbitrary as a board of director’s whim to either follow existing trends or break into new markets. However, this is only a single possible explanation, and more discussion is warranted as to why certain trends may be set, such exploration into the economic conditions or work culture of a region at certain times.

Genre Graphs

Compared to publishers, the differences are less immediately obvious. Within individual regions, we can sometimes associate large positive fluctuations with individual game releases, most obviously with Tetris, since its genre is otherwise rather unpopular.

A question that might come up often when thinking about genres and regional differences is “What is the underlying reason for certain regions’ preference for certain genres?” Some can be explained by culture, like Sports and Shooters, while others, like Japan’s Role-Playing Games are harder to pin down. Are Japanese Role-Playing Games a self-propagating trend after some initial breakout hit? Or is there instead some cultural reason that they like it more? What role do publishers purposefully play in setting and continuing genre trends? These questions are beyond the scope of the minimal history that can be displayed in our slide show and demand historical evidence to answer, but are interesting questions that warrant exploration nonetheless.
